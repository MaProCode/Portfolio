ONE-OFF SPORT CAR CLUSTER

Full featured Cluster development for a one-off Sport car (Corvette C8 based)

details:

- UI design according to customer specifications, and its implementation on commercial HW
- Data collection from vehicle CAN bus, queries on OBD port, and external sensors
- Electronic I/F development for external signals acquisitions
- Real time data display on cluster, with indication of:
vehicle speed, rpm, odometer, oil/water temperatures, oil pressure, battery level, fuel level, gear, door lock status, low/high beam, turn indicator, hanbrake, seat belt status
- custom graphical widget development for data display, and RPM bar indicator

-
Phase 1: Idea & Research
A feasibilty study was necessary to find and map all the signals required for the cluster indications
A Reverse Engineering analisys of the LIN / CAN OBD bus / CAN vehicle bus communication protocols, and the vehicle electrical interface has been conducted using Vector CANalyzer and lab instruments in order to detect all required signals. Several preliminary tests were conducted, aimed to acquire data and set commands.
This activity leads to obtain a large part of the GM vehicle CAN bus map to interact with.
Phase 2 - Design & UX
PC side development of a basic preliminary SW for the basic functions, including:

• Canonical cluster indicators:
> UI for various indicators
> custom graphical widget development for data display, and RPM bar indicator > Speed, odometer, gear shift, turn indicator, high/low light beam, handbrake signals acquisition
> Electric interface development to acquire analog signals from vehicle

A Demo mode was implemented as well, for an animated presentation of the UI to the customer.
Phase 3 - Prototyping
This phase involves the porting of the SW on Target board, adapting the UI to the display and hardware peripherals available on the board.
Prototype was framed in the stock car dashboard, to allow mechanical compatibility with the vehicle.
It was also the opportunity for a Boot time optimization, obtained overclocking the device, and disabling unused O.S. services.
Phase 4 - Testing
In Car integration and testing of the functionalities.
A specific electronic device has been developed and programmed to simulate car CAN bus communication lines (both diagnostic OBDII and vehicle GM line), in order to check the cluster functionalities and the UI responsiveness during the development phase, with the cluster standalone, disconnected by the car network.

Final check was made with the prototypal cluster integrated in vehicle, communicating with the real buses at effective speed.
Phase 5 - Final Result
Final phase foresee on the road and on track testing for the final parameter tuning and bug-fixing, validationg the project in the real environment according to a test case scheme.
Phase 6 - Devel Sixteen on media
Due to the iconic design and the extreme performance, the car has entered into the collective imagination, appearing in numerous reviews on the web, and articles on specialized automotive forums, and becoming part of the car fleet of various video games (Asphalt Legend, Assetto Corsa, and more).

It was also reviewed during an "on track" test, by SuperCar Blondie, an automotive blogger that posted a video on her youtube channel about this car.
In her video, the customized cluster is well visible in action.
