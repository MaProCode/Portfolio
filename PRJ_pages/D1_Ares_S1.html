CAR CLUSTER & INFOTAINMENT

Limited Edition sport car Cluster and Infotainment box

Full featured electronics development for a limited edition Sport car (Chevy Corvette C8 based), including a cluster, an infotainment and a passenger display, implementing customer design elements and functionalities.



details:

• Cluster includes accelerometer analog widget, engine map mode indication and all the classic cluster indicators.
• Infotainment has an Android Auto implementation (third party), Rear Camera, Tyre Pressure indication, HVAC driving capability via a touch screen and more.
• Passenger display is dedicated to a customized music player.

Phase 1: Idea & Research
Project Scope
This project regards the realization from the concept to the final product of the complete dashboard electronics, including a cluster, an infotainment box and a passenger display, for a limited-edition hypercar inspired by the racing prototypes of the 1970s and 80s.
The car combines extreme design with high performance and a focus on an unfiltered driving experience, and offer extensive customization possibilities to each client.
The production is limited, with only a small number of units, including a coupe and a speedster version, being made.



Preliminary activity
An on-vehicle CAN bus message reverse-engineering preliminary analysis was necessary, allowing us to understand and interpret a vehicle's communication network associated signals. This leads to obtain at the end, a great part of the GM CAN bus message mapping.

Preliminary tests include as well several checks to verify the integration between the choosen electronic platform and car electrical interface.
Phase 2 - Design & UX
PC side development of a basic preliminary SW for the basic functions, including:

• Canonical cluster indicators:
> UI with custom gadgets for various indicators
> Speed, odometer, gear shift, turn indicator, high/low light beam, handbrake
> Electric interface development to acquire analog signals from vehicle

• BMS functions:
> Battery voltage level, battery status, battery current absorption
> Battery pack OverTemperature / OverVoltage protections
> Mode selection commands for electric motor (Power mode/ECO mode)
> implementation of logic for range estimation

A Demo mode was implemented as well, for an animated presentation of the UI to the customer.
Phase 3 - Prototyping
This phase involves the porting of the SW on Target board, adapting the UI to the circular display.
It was also the opportunity for a Boot time optimization, obtained overclocking the device, and disabling unused O.S. services.
Phase 4 - Testing
In Car integration and testing of the functionalities.
BMS parameter tuning and optimization for odomenter, speed and range estimation.

Vehicle speed calculation is made via electric motor rpm detection, or using a pick-up installed on the gear shift axle, by means of conditioning electronic and pulse counting.
Odometer is calculated via a time-integration of the vehicle speed, using precise timer library, so to to obtain a precision estimated around 0.8%.
Phase 6 - Final Result
Final phase foresee on the road testing for the final parameter tuning and bug-fixing, validationg the project in the real environment according to a test case scheme.

One of the car equipped with this cluster, was sent to the Fiat 500 Museum located in Nagoya (Japan), that houses a collection of rare vintage Fiat cars. It is a reference point for Japanese enthusiasts of the Italian car and for those looking to purchase a restored model.
Phase 6 - Ares S1 on media
The ARES S1 is a limited-edition supercar by the Italian coachbuilder ARES Modena. It is a bespoke vehicle that combines the design language of a hypercar with the performance of a supercar. Due to it's Performance-Oriented specs, (it is powered by a powerful V8 engine), it's Exclusivity (only a small number of units are being built) and it's Distinctive Design featuring elements like a sculpted carbon fiber body and a unique silhouette, it easily drew attention among special car enthusiast audience, and motoring magazines.

It become part of the car fleet of various video games (Asphalt 9: Legend).

The car was also reviewed by several Web bloggers, in particular during an "on track" test, by Drive Up, a motor focused TV Show, and by by SuperCar Blondie, an automotive blogger, that posted several videos on Youtube about this car.
In some of these videos, the customized cluster / infotainment is well visible in action.
